Brazzaville

The small village of Mfoa, gaining importance with the establishment of a mission by the Spiritans, was erected in 1911 by the colonial administration as the commune of Brazzaville. It is named after the Italian-French explorer Pierre Savorgnan de Brazza. A first city hall was built in 1912 which was replaced in 1962-1963 by the current city hall. Its first coherent urban planning dates from 1929 under Governor General Antonetti.
